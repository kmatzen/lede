What Lede does

Lede reads notifications and message metadata from services you connect (currently Gmail, GitHub, Slack, Outlook, and Google/Outlook calendars), sends short snippets of that content to Anthropic's Claude API for scoring and summarization, and shows the result in a menu-bar panel.

Your responsibilities

  • Bring your own keys. Lede uses your Anthropic API key (or, in dev builds, your Claude subscription) for every triage call. You are responsible for those costs and for keeping the key out of other people's hands.
  • Honor the providers' terms. When you connect Gmail, GitHub, Slack, Outlook, or any other service, your use is governed by their terms — Lede doesn't alter or override them.
  • Use a Mac you control. Lede stores OAuth tokens in the macOS Keychain. Don't install it on a shared account where another user could use those tokens to access your data.
  • Don't redistribute under another name. The MIT license lets you fork and modify, but please don't ship a rebrand that confuses people about whether they're getting the real Lede.

What Lede is not responsible for

  • Third-party outages and changes. If Anthropic, Google, GitHub, Slack, or Microsoft change their APIs, rate-limit you, or go down, Lede may stop working until a new release lands. There's no SLA.
  • Triage accuracy. Claude's scoring is a heuristic — helpful in aggregate, occasionally wrong about a specific item. Don't rely on Lede as your only signal for time-critical messages.
  • Costs you incur on third-party services. Anthropic bills your account directly for API usage; the providers (Google, Microsoft, etc.) may impose their own quotas. Lede shows an estimate in the About pane but doesn't enforce limits.
  • Data you've already sent us. We don't have a server, so there's nothing for us to "delete on request." Disconnecting a source removes its tokens from your Keychain immediately; quitting Lede stops all activity; deleting the app removes the local cache.
